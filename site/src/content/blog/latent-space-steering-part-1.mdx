## Dime a Dozen?
I’m trying to come up with a hook for my intro to this blog post. Thankfully, that’s actually the point of the blog post.

It’s about leveraging AI to help automate the process of coming up with ideas. Not just any random “dime a dozen” ideas, but the sometimes subtle, yet unique insights which carry the potential to make a difference.

As <a href="https://medium.com/better-humans/how-ideas-are-formed-in-the-mind-e0607ae94097" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Dr Gabriel Barsawme said</a>, <EmojiTextFlashAnimation client:visible emoji="💡" color="#ca8a04" triggerAt={0.25}>“to be human is to come up with ideas”</EmojiTextFlashAnimation>.

And it’s not just a human quirk. Without new ideas, nothing moves forward! Not this blog post, not technology, not society; heck not even personal improvement can happen without them. We must always be connecting the dots; it’s the foundation of experimentation and discovery.

Some ideas change the world, like <SequentialTextGlow triggerAt={0.25} client:visible stepMs={450}>“what if <GlowSegment color="#22c55e">matter</GlowSegment> and <GlowSegment color="#3b82f6">energy</GlowSegment> are the <GlowSegment color="#ca8a04">same thing</GlowSegment>?”</SequentialTextGlow> (Einstein).

Sometimes ideas are far less earth-shattering, like “I should stop loading my dishwasher that way.”

But any idea can be valuable, big or small. If it calls out a new connection that leads to more effective behavior, it’s a good idea. In Einstein’s case, we understand the universe better. In my dishwasher case, I save money and time doing the dishes.

The way AI currently works is that we come to it with a request or thought. But what if, instead of you pinging your AI chatbot, it pings you sometimes, and says things like:

“Hey! I think I just realized how you could solve that problem with your difficult co-worker!”

Or

“<EmojiExpand client:visible emoji="💥" speed="fast" triggerAt={0.25}>OMG</EmojiExpand>. I got a new idea for your business - check this out…”

Or

“I was thinking about that war in the middle east; I wonder if anyone has considered this angle…”

Much like a friend who knows you and thinks about you even when you’re not together. It processes things when you’re not around, and comes up with new ideas, new insights, that may be helpful to you.

## <LightningStrike client:visible triggerAt={0.25}>The Spark</LightningStrike>

Exploring how AI can come up with new ideas is a topic very close to my heart. But of the myriad ways to try and move the needle on this topic, I opted for a fun, quick experiment to show whether we could do this quickly and cheaply. So let’s get into it.

How human ideation works is not a fully understood topic. But according to <a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5796649/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">this article</a>, a lot of evidence is saying that, to come up with new ideas, our brains “integrate memory content in new ways.” That is, memories or thoughts that previously were unconnected, begin to connect. My human experience supports this.

I’ve tried to force AI to do this, and sometimes it comes up with a great idea. But in my experience, this is **always** because I gave it multiple pieces of information that it drew a new insight out of. In other words, I have to show up with the beginnings of an idea, usually by presenting two different concepts that are loosely related. I provide the spark.

As an example, I present to you my highly contrived scenario: Say that I love cars. Say I also have budget issues. Now, if I just told an AI that I have budget issues, it would give me generic budgeting advice. But if instead I told it that I like cars, and added that I also have budget issues… it would consider those things potentially related and come up with the idea that I may be spending too much on cars.

We have to bridge the gaps for AI to realize there may be a connection there. We have to provide the spark.

And we need to give AI a way to provide its own spark.

What if I woke up and my AI tapped me on the shoulder and said, “Jeff, you told me awhile back that you love cars. Yesterday you also said you’re struggling with your budget. Let me ask you this… are you buying a lot of cars? Because that might be your problem.”

That’s what I’m after. And I want a ***cheap, simple*** way to go about getting AI to do this.
